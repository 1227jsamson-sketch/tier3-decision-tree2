Tier 3 Strategic Exception Decision Tree

Quick reference cheat sheet to determine whether a concern qualifies for Tier 3 Strategic Exception handling and the typical resolution type.

1

Can You Resolve the Concern Using Standard Policy?

Start by determining whether the issue can be resolved with your normal authority and existing procedures.

✅ Yes

Tier 3? No

Resolution: Follow standard policy and procedures.

❌ No

Tier 3? Yes — Continue to Step 2.

Resolution: Requires strategic exception review.

2

Identify the Exception Category

Match the guest concern to the appropriate Tier 3 category.

⚖️ Legal Exposure

Injury, ADA, discrimination, legal threats

  • Guest reports an injury during the stay
  • Guest alleges discrimination
  • Guest requests an incident report
  • "If this isn't resolved, I will contact a lawyer."

Tier 3? Yes

Typical Resolution: Refund or Credit

💳 System Failure

Double charge, booking error

  • Duplicate charge
  • Booking system error
  • Website malfunction
  • Incorrect reservation details caused by the system

Tier 3? Yes

Typical Resolution: Refund Required

📱 Reputational Risk

Social or influencer threat

  • Guest threatens to post on social media
  • Guest references influencer status
  • Guest says they will leave negative reviews

Tier 3? Yes

Typical Resolution: Refund or Credit

⚠️ Policy Breakdown

Agent error, wrong information given

  • Another representative provided incorrect information
  • Agent promised a resolution not supported by policy
  • Operational or policy error created the issue

Tier 3? Yes

Typical Resolution: Refund or Credit

3

Review Guest Case History

Strategic exceptions require a review of previous guest contacts and unresolved concerns.

Has the guest contacted via voice, chat, or web multiple times?
Has the concern remained unresolved?
What is the guest's loyalty status and lifetime value?
When was the last exception granted?

Quick Decision Table

Actual Scenario Tier 3? Typical Resolution

Duplicate charge or booking error Yes Refund Required
Social media or influencer threat Yes Refund or Credit
Agent error or wrong information given Yes Refund or Credit
Cancellation within standard policy No Standard Refund

🐺 Rule of Thumb

If the concern falls outside standard policy, involves legal, operational, or reputational risk, and requires manual review or higher-level approval, it should be escalated to Tier 3 Strategic Exception handling.
